About Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ)

BJJ is a martial art designed to submit a bigger, stronger more athletic opponent without punching or kicking them.

What is BJJ?

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a grappling-based martial art that focuses on ground fighting and submission holds. It emphasizes technique over strength, allowing smaller practitioners to effectively defend against larger opponents.

Training Methods

Training involves both gi (traditional uniform) and no-gi practice, with emphasis on live sparring (rolling) to develop practical skills. Classes typically include warm-ups, technique instruction, drilling, and live training.

Core Principles

Leverage Over Strength

Using proper technique and mechanical advantage to overcome physical disparities.

Position Before Submission

Establishing dominant control positions before attempting submission holds.

Efficiency of Movement

Using minimal energy while maximizing effectiveness through proper technique.

Training Options

Gi Training

Traditional BJJ training wearing the gi (kimono), focusing on grips and technical details.

No-Gi Training

Training without the gi, wearing rashguards and shorts, emphasizing body control and wrestling.
